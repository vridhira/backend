# Payment Schedule - Commercial Invoicing and Validity Rules

This document governs invoicing and payment mechanics for approved commercial
licensees under a signed COMMERCIAL-LICENSE-AGREEMENT.md.

No signed agreement means no commercial payment plan and no commercial rights.

---

## 1) Commercial Invoicing Model

- Commercial licenses are billed via custom invoicing prior to the commencement
  of the License Term.
- Invoice amount, currency, tax treatment, and payment channel are defined per
  approved scope.
- Commercial use may start only after invoice settlement and written activation
  by Licensor.

---

## 2) Initial Term Billing

- Initial commercial term is twelve (12) months unless otherwise specified in a
  signed agreement.
- Full payment for the initial term is due before term start date.
- Partial access, phased go-live, or staged rollout billing is valid only if
  explicitly written in the signed agreement.

---

## 3) Renewal Billing Rules

- Renewals do not occur automatically.
- Renewal invoices must be paid thirty (30) days prior to the current term
  expiration date.
- If renewal payment is not received and confirmed by that deadline, renewal is
  not granted and commercial rights lapse at expiration.

---

## 4) Non-Payment Effect

Failure to pay any due commercial invoice invalidates the commercial license
immediately.

Upon invalidation, Licensee must:

- immediately stop all commercial use
- disconnect software from revenue-generating environments
- follow the post-termination obligations in the signed agreement

---

## 6) Payment Confirmation and Records

Licensee must provide payment confirmation details upon transfer, including:

- legal entity name
- invoice number
- payment date
- transaction reference
- remittance amount

Licensor may request supporting remittance records for reconciliation.

---

## 7) Taxes and Duties

Unless expressly stated otherwise in a signed agreement, Licensee is
responsible for applicable taxes, withholding, and statutory payment duties
associated with invoice settlement.

---

## 8) Disputes and Holds

Invoice disputes must be raised in writing before the due date.
Undisputed amounts remain payable on time.
Dispute submission does not grant continued commercial use beyond paid term.

---

## 9) Precedence

If this schedule conflicts with a fully executed commercial agreement, the
executed agreement controls for that Licensee.
